Activated Carbon for COD and Color Removal in Textile Wastewater

Textile wastewater can contain reactive dyes, disperse dyes, surfactants, sizing agents and finishing chemicals. Biological treatment removes part of the load, but residual color and recalcitrant COD often require a polishing step. Activated carbon is one of the most common polishing options.

Why Activated Carbon Works

Activated carbon removes many dye molecules and organic residues through adsorption. The useful surface area is controlled by pore structure, surface chemistry and particle form. Coal-based and coconut-shell carbons are both used, but the best grade depends on dye size, COD profile, pH and contact time.

Powdered vs Granular Carbon

FormTypical useStrength
Powdered activated carbonBatch or contact-tank dosingFlexible for shock loads and seasonal color spikes
Granular activated carbonFixed-bed polishing after biological treatmentBetter for continuous, stable effluent polishing

Operating Checks

  • Track both COD and color; they do not always break through at the same time.
  • Confirm pH because dye adsorption can change sharply outside the neutral range.
  • Measure contact time and bed loading before comparing carbon grades.
  • Use actual effluent for bench adsorption tests before full-scale purchase.
