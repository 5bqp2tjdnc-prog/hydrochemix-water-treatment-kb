Activated Carbon for Drinking Water Polishing

Activated carbon is often used in drinking water polishing to reduce taste, odor, residual organics, and certain trace contaminants. The right grade depends on the target compounds, contact time, filtration design, particle size, and pressure drop limits.

For procurement, buyers should review iodine value, methylene blue value where relevant, ash content, moisture, hardness, particle size distribution, and leachable impurities. Drinking water applications also require careful review of safety documentation and local compliance needs.

Pilot testing or column testing is useful when the water source changes seasonally or when specific odor and organic targets must be controlled. Activated carbon should be selected for adsorption performance and operating life, not only initial purchase cost.
