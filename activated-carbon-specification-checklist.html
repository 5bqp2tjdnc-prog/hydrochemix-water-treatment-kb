Activated Carbon Specification Checklist for Water Treatment Buyers

Use this checklist when comparing powdered activated carbon, granular activated carbon, coconut shell activated carbon and coal-based activated carbon for water treatment projects.

Application Questions

  • Is the carbon used for drinking water, process water, wastewater polishing, odor control, color reduction, dechlorination or industrial adsorption?
  • Is the process batch dosing, continuous filtration, fixed-bed adsorption or emergency treatment?
  • Will the system use powdered activated carbon or granular activated carbon?
  • Is upstream pretreatment enough to control suspended solids, oil, turbidity or biological fouling?
  • What is the target result: taste, odor, color, COD/TOC polishing, chlorine removal, trace organics or equipment protection?

Specification Items To Compare

  • Raw material: coconut shell, coal-based, wood-based or other source.
  • Iodine value.
  • Methylene blue value if color removal is important.
  • Mesh size or particle size distribution.
  • Ash content, moisture, pH and bulk density.
  • Hardness and abrasion resistance for GAC.
  • Fines, dust level, washing level and leachable impurities.
  • Packaging, shelf life, TDS, SDS, COA and export documents.

Testing Notes

For drinking water polishing, check ash, pH, fines, leachable impurities, taste impact and required compliance documents. For wastewater color and odor polishing, methylene blue value, pore distribution and real water testing may be more useful than iodine value alone.

For GAC filters, confirm empty bed contact time, bed depth, flow rate, pressure drop, backwashing and breakthrough monitoring. For powdered activated carbon dosing, confirm dose range, mixing time, downstream solids removal, dust control and sludge impact.
