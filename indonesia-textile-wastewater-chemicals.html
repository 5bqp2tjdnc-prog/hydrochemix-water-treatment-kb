Water Treatment Chemicals for Indonesia Textile Wastewater

Textile wastewater in Indonesia may contain dyes, auxiliaries, surfactants, salts, starch, and suspended solids. Chemical treatment is usually designed to reduce color, turbidity, and part of the organic load before biological treatment, filtration, or discharge polishing.

PAC is commonly assessed for coagulation and color reduction, while PAM can improve floc strength and settling. The best result depends on wastewater type, dye chemistry, pH adjustment, mixing intensity, and retention time in the treatment system.

Procurement teams should avoid choosing chemicals by generic grade names alone. A controlled jar test can compare dosage, floc size, supernatant clarity, sludge volume, and the stability of the treated water after standing.
