Jar Test Procedure for Coagulant Optimization

A jar test is the standard bench procedure for comparing coagulant dose, pH, mixing energy and floc settling before changing a plant set point. It is useful for PAC, alum, ferric chloride, PAFC and polymer-assisted coagulation.

Basic Workflow

  1. Collect a representative raw-water or wastewater sample and record pH, turbidity, temperature and alkalinity.
  2. Prepare a dose series across six beakers, keeping sample volume and mixing time consistent.
  3. Run rapid mix, slow flocculation and settling stages.
  4. Measure settled turbidity, color, pH and visible floc quality.
  5. Repeat around the best dose with smaller increments.

What to Record

FieldWhy it matters
Coagulant doseDefines the treatment curve and chemical cost.
pH and alkalinityControls hydrolysis and residual metal risk.
Floc size and settlingPredicts clarifier and filter performance.
Residual turbidity/colorShows whether the dose meets the treatment target.

Common Mistakes

  • Testing settled or aged samples instead of fresh representative water.
  • Changing pH and coagulant dose at the same time without a matrix.
  • Over-mixing polymer and destroying flocculant performance.
  • Choosing the lowest turbidity dose without checking sludge, pH and residual metal.
