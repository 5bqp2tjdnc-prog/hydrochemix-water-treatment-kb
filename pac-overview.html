PAC Overview: Poly Aluminium Chloride for Water Treatment

Poly aluminium chloride (PAC) is the most widely used inorganic coagulant in modern water treatment. Compared with traditional aluminium sulphate, PAC offers faster floc formation, larger floc size, wider pH tolerance and lower sludge volume.

What is PAC?

PAC is a group of aluminium hydroxychloride polymers with the general formula [Al2(OH)nCl6-n]m. The degree of polymerisation and basicity determine its coagulation performance. It is available as yellow or light brown powder, or as a liquid solution.

Key Quality Parameters

  • Al2O3 content: Active ingredient, typically 28–30% for powder products.
  • Basicity: Degree of polymerisation, affects floc size and settling speed.
  • Water-insoluble matter: Indicates purity; important for drinking water applications.
  • pH (1% solution): Usually 3.5–5.0, reflecting acidic nature.
  • Heavy metals: Arsenic, lead, cadmium limits apply for drinking water grades.

Common Applications

PAC is used across municipal drinking water treatment, industrial wastewater clarification, textile wastewater decolorization, paper mill effluent treatment, mining wastewater and sludge thickening. It works by neutralising the negative charge on suspended particles, allowing them to aggregate into larger, settleable flocs.

How to Choose the Right PAC

Selecting PAC is not simply a price comparison. Buyers should match the grade to the application: drinking water projects need NSF-certified or food-grade material; industrial wastewater may benefit from higher basicity formulations. The best way to confirm performance is a jar test using actual water samples.
