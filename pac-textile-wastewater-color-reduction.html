PAC for Textile Wastewater Color Reduction

PAC can be useful in textile wastewater color reduction when dyes and suspended matter can be destabilized through coagulation. Performance depends strongly on dye type, wastewater pH, alkalinity, salinity, and the presence of surfactants or auxiliaries.

A practical test should compare more than treated water appearance. Operators should record PAC dosage, mixing conditions, settling time, sludge volume, pH after dosing, and whether the clarified water affects downstream biological or filtration units.

For purchasing, buyers should check alumina content, basicity, insoluble matter, packaging, shelf life, COA, and SDS. The most suitable PAC is not always the strongest grade on paper; it is the grade that performs consistently in the real wastewater stream.
