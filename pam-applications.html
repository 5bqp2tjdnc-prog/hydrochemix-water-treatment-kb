PAM Applications: Polyacrylamide Across Industries

Polyacrylamide (PAM) is a versatile water-soluble polymer used as a flocculant, thickener, binder and friction reducer across multiple industries. Its performance depends on ionic type (anionic, cationic, nonionic), molecular weight and charge density.

Sludge Dewatering

Sludge dewatering is the most common PAM application. Cationic PAM is widely used in municipal wastewater treatment plants for dewatering activated sludge and anaerobic digested sludge. It improves cake solids, reduces polymer dosage and lowers filtrate turbidity when applied before belt filter presses or centrifuges.

Wastewater Clarification

Both anionic and nonionic PAM serve as flocculant aids in primary and secondary clarification. When added after coagulants like PAC or ferric chloride, PAM bridges small flocs into larger, faster-settling aggregates. This reduces settling time and improves effluent quality.

Mining and Mineral Processing

In mining operations, anionic PAM is used for tailings thickening, coal washing, and mineral processing water recycling. High-molecular-weight anionic PAM is particularly effective for solid-liquid separation in iron ore, coal and gold mining operations.

How to Select the Right PAM

The key to effective PAM selection is matching the charge type and density to the target particles. Organic sludges generally respond well to cationic PAM, while inorganic mineral suspensions typically work better with anionic or nonionic grades. Molecular weight affects floc size and shear resistance.

Always test multiple PAM grades through jar testing or on-site trials before full-scale adoption. Dosage optimisation is critical — both under-dosing and over-dosing reduce performance.
