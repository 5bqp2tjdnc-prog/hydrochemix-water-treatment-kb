PAM for Sludge Dewatering Projects

PAM selection for sludge dewatering should be based on sludge source, solids concentration, organic content, pH, and the dewatering equipment used. Belt presses, centrifuges, screw presses, and filter presses may require different polymer behavior.

The key choice is not only molecular weight. Charge type, charge density, dissolution quality, aging time, and dosing point all affect floc strength and water release. Poor preparation can make a suitable PAM grade look ineffective during site testing.

A practical trial should measure polymer consumption, filtrate clarity, cake moisture, throughput, and equipment stability. Buyers should also confirm product documentation, safe handling requirements, and whether the grade is suitable for the intended sludge stream.
