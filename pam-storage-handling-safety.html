PAM Storage and Handling Safety Guide

Polyacrylamide (PAM) is widely used as a flocculant for wastewater clarification and sludge dewatering. It is effective when stored dry, dissolved correctly and dosed gently. Most field problems come from moisture exposure, poor make-down, high shear or slippery spills.

Dry Product Storage

  • Store unopened bags in a dry, ventilated warehouse.
  • Keep bags away from direct sunlight, rain, washdown areas and humid floors.
  • Seal opened bags and use them quickly to avoid moisture pickup and lump formation.
  • Track batch numbers and first-opened dates for each pallet.

Make-Down and Dosing

PAM should be wetted gradually and aged before dosing. A typical working solution is prepared at low concentration, mixed slowly and allowed to mature before use. High-speed mixing or centrifugal pumping can shear the polymer chain and reduce floc strength.

StepGood practice
WettingIntroduce powder evenly into moving water; avoid dumping powder into a tank.
AgingAllow enough maturation time before dosing to process water.
PumpingUse low-shear transfer where possible.
Spill responseSweep dry powder first; contain hydrated solution with absorbent material.

Safety Notes

Dry PAM dust can irritate airways, and hydrated PAM creates a serious slip hazard. Operators should use eye protection, gloves, suitable footwear and dust protection during bag cutting or hopper loading.
