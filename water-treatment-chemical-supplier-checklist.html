Water Treatment Chemical Supplier Selection Checklist

Choosing a water treatment chemical supplier is not only a price decision. Product consistency, documentation, logistics, application support and batch traceability all affect plant performance and compliance risk.

Core Evaluation Areas

AreaWhat to check
QualityCOA on every batch, stable active content, insolubles, pH and traceability.
ComplianceSDS, ISO certificates, NSF/ANSI 60 where drinking water use applies, local regulatory documents.
Technical supportJar testing, dose optimization, troubleshooting and application notes.
LogisticsLead time, emergency supply, packaging, tanker compatibility and minimum order quantity.
Total costDose, sludge handling, storage, equipment wear and delivery cost, not unit price alone.

Questions for Shortlisted Suppliers

  • Can you provide recent COA and SDS documents for the exact grade?
  • Can you run jar tests on our actual water sample?
  • What is the normal lead time and emergency delivery path?
  • Which plants with similar water quality already use this grade?
  • How do you handle off-specification product or logistics failures?

Procurement Decision

Score each supplier against the same weighted checklist. For drinking water, compliance documentation and product consistency usually carry the highest weight. For industrial wastewater, dose response, sludge impact and technical support often decide the real cost.
